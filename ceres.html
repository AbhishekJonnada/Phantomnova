Ceres - A Gaint Asteroid

About

Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter and is classified as a dwarf planet. It was discovered by Italian astronomer Giuseppe Piazzi in 1801. Ceres has a diameter of about 590 miles (940 kilometers) and accounts for approximately 40% of the mass of the entire asteroid belt. It was originally classified as an asteroid but was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006 by the International Astronomical Union (IAU). Ceres is unique among the dwarf planets in our solar system because it is the only one located in the inner solar system, not in the outer regions. It is believed to have a rocky core surrounded by a thick mantle of water ice, which may contain more fresh water than all of Earth's fresh water sources combined. Ceres also features a mysterious bright spot in the Occator Crater, which is thought to be deposits of salt or other reflective materials.
